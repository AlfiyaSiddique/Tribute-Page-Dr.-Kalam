Dr.A.P.J Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man Of India

Introduction

Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam's full name was Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam. He was born on 15 October 1931 and died on 27 July 2015. He was an Indian Aerospace scientist and a politician who played a leading role in the development of India's missile programs and space programs.He had also served India as the 11th President and he used to called as 'The People's President', widely because of his amazing thoughts and decisions for the welfare of the citizens.India has given the title of 'The Missile Man Of India' to Dr. Kalam .

Dr. kalam, bottle-feeding an injured fawn at Rastriyapati Bhavan(president's House).

Biography

  • 1931- Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 in the state of Tamil Nadu, India.
  • 1947- Dr. Kalam compleated his higher school education from 'Schwartz Higher Secondary School' in Ramanathapuram and then went to attend Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli,
  • 1954- He compleated his graduation from the University Of Madras in Physics .
  • 1960- Graduated from the Madrass Institute Of Technology in Aerospace Engeneering and Then went to Defence Research & Development Service (DRDS) as a scientist.
  • 1965- He started working on expandable rocket project independently at DRDO.
  • 1969- Dr. kalam was transfered to Indian Space Research Organisation , where he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III)
  • 1963 to 1964- He visited NASA's Langley Research Center in Hampton, Virginia; Goddard Space Flight Center in Greenbelt, Maryland; and Wallops Flight Facility.
  • 1970 to 1990-
    1. Kalam made an effort to develop the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and SLV-III projects, both of which proved to be successful.
    2. In the 1970s, Kalam also directed two projects, Project Devil and Project Valiant , which sought to develop ballistic missiles from the technology of the successful SLV programme
    3. His research and educational leadership brought him great laurels and prestige in the 1980s, which prompted the government to initiate an advanced missile programme under his directorship.
  • 1991 to 2000-
    1. Kalam served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation from July 1992 to December 1999.
    2. In 1998, along with cardiologist Soma Raju, Kalam developed a low cost coronary stent, named the "Kalam-Raju Stent" .
  • 2002 to 2007- He Served as the 11th president of India . He was also the first scientist and the first bachelor to occupy Rashtrapati Bhawan.
  • 2012- In May 2012, Kalam launched a programme for the youth of India called the What Can I Give Movement, with a central theme of defeating corruption.
  • 2015- Died on 27 July 2015. His last words, to his aide Srijan Pal Singh, were reportedly: "Funny guy! Are you doing well?"
